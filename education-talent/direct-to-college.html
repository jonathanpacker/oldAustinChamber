Direct-to-College Achievement Plan

The Direct-to-College Achievement Plan, or DTC70, is the first multi-chamber, multi-school district, multi-business compact to commit to enroll 70% of the Class of 2018-2019 directly in higher education.

Direct-to-College 70 (DTC 70)

Our public schools have achieved record regional graduation and college career-readiness rates. Direct-to-college enrollment rates improved significantly between 2002-2008, then leveled at 62% (±1%), lagging the national direct-to-college enrollment rate. At the same time, roughly 55% of local job openings require at least an Associate's degree, or greater, and 60% of our unemployed lack these credentials. Higher direct-to-college enrollment rates will mean more economic opportunity, a higher standard of living, and lower rates of intergenerational poverty for our community. 

20+ business and education leaders signed a comprehensive agreement to resource and accomplish the DTC70 goal. 

The Chamber and business leadership committed to:

  • Identify the strategies which increase college going rates;
  • Coordinate marketing and efforts to help families complete the college financial aid process;
  • Advocate for policies which support DTC70 goals;
  • Communicate, track and recognize ISD/partner progress on DTC70 goals on a monthly/bi-weekly basis.

Five school districts commit to:

  • Set college readiness and direct-to-college enrollment targets to meet a 70% DTC rate for Class of 2019;
  • Review student-to-counselor ratios to improve college/career guidance and support college enrollment for students;
  • Add activities needed for college/career enrollment. curriculum K-12 courses;
  • Make student data more accessible to counselors to advice students.

ACC committed to:

  • Provide college readiness, college application support, financial aid support to ISD partners and students;
  • Share key college-going data with counselors to target college transition support to students;
  • Explore establishing a permanent college support center at ACC's Highland Mall Campus or other central location;
  • Help prepare high school teachers to teach dual credit courses on high school campuses

Master Community Workforce Plan

The Austin Chamber of Commerce Board of Directors applauds the Workforce Solutions Capital Area Board of Directors for adopting its first Master Workforce Plan for 2017- 2021. The plan's top-line target is to ensure an additional 1,800 Austin residents currently in poverty receive training/credentialing and are placed in jobs paying at least $40,000. The plan identifies high-level strategies to raise awareness of workforce training opportunities, increase training program success rates and to improve job placement rates in fields of training.
