Financial Aid Saturdays

The Financial Aid Saturdays Program provides FREE assistance to Central Texas high school seniors, prospective/current college students, and parents/guardians needing help to complete federal and state financial aid applications to fund college enrollment.

Chamber Financial Aid Saturday Volunteer Training

Chamber business, school district, higher education and community volunteers can sign up to assist students and parents with filling out the FAFSA or TASFA during the Chamber's Financial Aid events.

To qualify, volunteers must attend a single, 1.5 hour, financial aid training session over lunch provided by the Austin Chamber and facilitated by TG. All trainings are scheduled from 11:30 am to 1 pm with lunch provided.

View dates and sign up for a training session!

ApplyTexas

In accordance with DTC70, nine Central Texas school districts will attempt to enroll 70% of their 2016 graduates directly into college. To accomplish this objective, about 88% of Central Texas students will need to submit the ApplyTexas common college application. The Texas Common Application is accepted by every Texas Public University, as well as participating community and private colleges. Students can use the same saved application to apply to multiple schools. Click on the most recent report below to see how each high school is performing.

FAFSA

As we say, "Timing is College Money", filing for financial aid early (before March 31) means getting the most money for college. The nine school districts in the DTC70 partnership have set a 55% FAFSA submission goal by March 31st. If this is accomplished, Central Texas will see a significant rise in the amount of students able to enroll in college.
