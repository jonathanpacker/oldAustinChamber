  • Global Gateway

Austin Global Gateway works with foreign investors and businesses who are interested in investing in or relocating to the Austin, Texas region.

Austin in the News

"Overseas Investors Could be Lucrative Source of Funding for Austin Companies," Austin Business Journal, 8 August 2018.

Overview + Mission

Austin Global Gateway (AGG) Advisory Board members are Austin-based business leaders with extensive international experience. They have worked and had management responsibilities for markets in Asia, Europe, the Middle East, and Latin America. They believe in the value of personal contacts in establishing strong and trusting relationships. In their regular or AGG-planned international travel, they meet with prominent business leaders to promote Austin as a city warranting close examination for their expansion strategies. They welcome foreign investors and business executives who wish to examine Austin more closely. Committee members assist in introducing foreign executives to their counterparts in Austin who are in line with their business operations and investment goals.

AGG is a program of the Greater Austin Chamber of Commerce.

As a business hub, Austin, Texas is one of the most dynamic cities in the United States. It is a leader in technological innovation, quality of its workforce, and corporate friendly environment. Over the recent five-year period, real per capita GDP grew by 16.6% to $135 billion, making Austin the fourth best performing major metro in the U.S. Austin ranked seventh in the JLL City Momentum Index of 30 cities worldwide. Austin’s current GDP per capita of $61,183 is forecasted to grow to $65,858 by 2021.

Austin was named the "Best Place to Live" in U.S. News and World Report's annual ranking in 2018 and 2017. It is also the capital of Texas, a state with a GDP of $1.4 trillion.

Its cost of living, location near the geographical middle of the U.S., quality of office space and educational facilities, access to quality workforce, climate, and domestic and international air travel linkages, have been reasons for an increasing number of U.S. corporations to establish subsidiaries or headquarters in the Austin area.

AGG acknowledges that while Austin has already established its status for domestic U.S. corporations, the city’s reputation among foreign business leaders has lagged. The AGG executive committee endeavors to correct this disparity by leveraging its member relationships to demonstrate Austin’s comparative advantage to discriminating investors, creative entrepreneurs, and dynamic corporations worldwide.

Foreign Trade & Investment

  • Texas is the country’s largest exporter and has been ranked as the top exporter since 2002.
  • Austin exported $12.5 billion worth of goods in 2017.
  • The region’s leading trading partners are Taiwan, South Korea, China, Japan, and Mexico.
  • The Austin metropolitan area attracts a significant foreign direct investment (FDI) from companies across the globe.
  • Austin garners 11% of the state’s FDI.
  • Over the last five years, Austin was the number three city in Texas for the total number of FDI projects.
  • Austin is number one for semiconductor and electronics and number two for software and information technology FDI projects in Texas.
