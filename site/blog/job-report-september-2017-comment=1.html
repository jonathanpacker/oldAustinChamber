AUSTIN JOB POSTINGS REPORT SEPTEMBER 2017

AUSTIN - ROUND ROCK - SAN MARCOS MSA
(AUGUST 14, 2017 to SEPTEMBER 13, 2017)
austinchamber.com/jobpostings

(NOT FOR CITATION OR CIRCULATION WITHOUT PERMISSION)

INSIGHTS

  • 39,500 Available Job Openings, but fewer than a year ago. 39,500 Available Job Postings during September 2017, or 1,600 less compared to September 2016.

  • Increase in tech, business and financial operations occupations. The Computer and Mathematical Occupational Group and the Business and Financial Operations Occupational Group have the largest year-to-year Available Job Posting increases.

  • Job postings in September 2017 continues to be lower than September of past three years. The Austin MSA saw more than 40,000 Available Job Postings in September of 2014, 2015 and 2016 but is below 40,000 in September 2017.

  • Demand for Tech talent remains high. Eight of the Top 20 occupations are tech-related. Registered Nurses is the top posted occupation currently for September 2017 while Computer Systems Analysts saw the largest year-to-year increase in postings for all occupations posted and is not a too difficult-to-hire occupation in our region.

  • Plenty of middle wage jobs available. Half of the Top 20 occupations posted require less than an associate's degree as the typical education needed for entry, representing about 6,100 postings. All but two, Retail Salesperson and Maids and Housekeeping Cleaners, posted an annual median wage of $30,000 or more in the Austin MSA. Almost one-fifth of all Available Job Postings that require at least a high school diploma, also pays an annual median wage of between $30,000 to $60,000. Two-fifths of all Available Job Postings require no formal credential or high school equivalency as the Typical Education Needed for Entry, though most of these would require short-term/moderate-term on-the job training and/or related work experience. About five percent of Available Job Postings would require an Associate's degree as the Typical Education Needed for Entry, almost all do not require additional work experience nor on-the-job training.

  • Unemployment tops 37,800. According to the Texas Workforce Commission, the Austin MSA reported an unemployment rate of 3.4% or 37,800 unemployed in August 2017. 

  • More jobs ahead. Companies recently announced new location or expansion plans in the Austin MSA, representing about 295 jobs. The list includes Acessa Health, Convey, Granite & Stone, Kestra Financial, Namely, Veggie Noodle Company and Vyze (formerly NewComLink).

The following job market report is designed to provide insight into current employer demands, skills and occupations in the Austin MSA.

There were approximately 39,500 Available Job Postings during the September 2017 monthly reporting period, a decrease of about 1,600 Available Job Postings compared to September 2016. About 7,400 or 19 percent of the Available Job Postings during the September reporting period fell under Computer and Mathematical Occupations. 48 percent or 19,000 are New Job Postings, the lowest for the month of September when compared to September 2013 through September 2016, which were above 20,000.

The Major Occupational Groups that saw the largest decline in the volume of Available Job Postings compared to September 2016 are Healthcare Practitioners and Technical Occupations, followed by Food Preparation and Serving Related Occupations, Office and Administrative Support Occupations and Sales and Related Occupations. 

According to the Texas Workforce Commission, the Austin MSA reported an unemployment rate of 3.4% or 37,800 unemployed in August 2017. This represents an increase of 1,400 unemployed compared to July 2017 which had a 3.2% unemployment rate or 36,400 unemployed. Approximately 63% of unemployment insurance (UI) claimants that are actively seeking a job in Travis County have less than an associate's degree, according to Workforce Solutions Capital Area.

TOP 20 JOB POSTINGS

The chart below lists the top 20 unique available job postings in the Austin – Round Rock – San Marcos MSA from August 14, 2017 to September 13, 2017.

Source: The Conference Board Help Wanted OnLine ® (HWOL) and Bureau of Labor Statistics, May 2016

Computer Systems Analysts, Management Analysts, Information Technology Project Managers, Computer Systems Engineers/Architects and Marketing Managers, Retail Salesperson and Social and Human Service Assistants all saw a 80 or more increase in year-to-year postings. Web Developers, Registered Nurses, Nursing Assistants, Customer Service Representatives, Demonstrators and Product Promoters and First-Line Supervisors of Food Preparation and Serving Workers had a 100 or more decrease in year-to-year postings.

TOP 20 JOB POSTINGS AND HIRING DIFFICULTY

The chart below lists the top 20 unique available job postings and its hiring difficulty in the Austin – Round Rock – San Marcos MSA from August 14, 2017 to September 13, 2017.


Source: Wanted Technologies and the Bureau of Labor Statistics

The Hiring Scale views the difficulty-to-fill indicator on market conditions. It calculates a score for each position that the employer is trying to fill. Several factors affect the hiring scale score that include the supply of available candidates, the overall demand for employers for people in the position, the location, the occupation, local unemployment rates, job site source, offered pay level, etc. The Hiring Score scores jobs from 1 to 99 based on their projected difficulty-to-recruit.

Half of the Top 20 occupations posted require less than an associate's degree as the typical education needed for entry. All but two, Retail Salesperson and Maids and Housekeeping Cleaners, posted an annual median wage of $30,000 or more in the Austin MSA.

When compared nationwide, Executive Secretaries and Executive Administrative Assistants, Computer Systems Analysts, Heavy and Tractor-Trailer Truck Drivers and Computer User Support Specialists are the easier-to-hire occupations in the Austin MSA. Maids and Housekeeping Cleaners, Registered Nurses, Maintenance and Repair Workers, General, Management Analysts and First-Line Supervisors of Retail Sales Workers are the job postings that more difficult to fill in our region. Approximately 46 percent of all Available Job Postings require a Bachelor's degree or more as the entry level of educational qualification needed for the posted occupation.

Registered Nurses and Critical Care Nurses saw the largest decline in Available Job Postings.

Healthcare Practitioners and Technical Occupations saw approximately 800 less Available Job Postings compared to the same period last year. In terms of New Job Postings, Registered Nurses, Radiologic Technologists and Surgical Technologists saw a decline of 30 or more year-to-year postings.
