AUSTIN JOB POSTINGS REPORT SEPTEMBER 2016

AUSTIN - ROUND ROCK - SAN MARCOS MSA
(AUGUST 14, 2016 to SEPTEMBER 13, 2016)
austinchamber.com/jobpostings
The following job market report is designed to provide insight into current employer demands, skills and occupations in the Austin MSA.

INSIGHTS

  • Approximately 40,000 Available Job Postings during the monthly reporting period, a decrease of 5,000 postings compared to the same period last year. 
  • Largest year-to-year decreases in the Computer and Mathematical Occupations, followed by Healthcare Practitioners and Technical Occupations and Sales and Related Occupations.
  • Several companies recently announced new location or expansion plans in Austin, involving more than 340 jobs. The list includes Aliya Financial, Able Design Group, SpeachMe, SolarCity, Innowave RF, Hello Soda, Deep Eddy Vodka, Cognitive Scale, CipherLoc and Aceable.

There were approximately 40,000 Available Job Postings during the September 2016 monthly reporting period, a decrease of about 5,000 Available Job Postings compared to September of 2015. About 6,800 or 17 percent of the available postings during the September reporting period fell under Computer and Mathematical Occupations.

Other occupational groups not reflected in this report that accounts for another 5,100 Available Job Openings are Building and Grounds Cleaning and Maintenance Occupations (3%), Education, Training, and Library Occupations (3%), Architecture and Engineering Occupations (3%), Healthcare Support Occupations (3%) and Arts, Design, Entertainment, Sports, and Media Occupations (2%).

USA Today ranked Austin 9th on the "American Cities Adding The Most Jobs". The Kauffman Foundation named Austin 1st in the "Top 25 Cities For Startup Activity, 2016". According to CBRE, Austin is ranked 5th in the "Scoring Tech Talent" list for 2016.

According to the Texas Workforce Commission, the Austin MSA reported an unemployment rate of 3.5% or 38,700 unemployed in August 2016. The Austin MSA reported an annual job growth rate of 3.2% or 30,900 net new jobs in August 2016, making Austin the tenth fastest growing major metro in the nation. Professional and Business Services added the most jobs (6,800 or 4.2%) in the past year. The fastest growing industries are Construction and Natural Resources (12.3%) and Wholesale Trade (7.6%).

Approximately 65% of unemployment insurance (UI) claimants that are actively seeking a job in Travis County have less than an associate's degree, according to Workforce Solutions Capital Area.

Source: The Conference Board Help Wanted OnLine ® (HWOL)

Note: The data for September 2016 would be the sum of all posted ads from August 14, 2016 to September 13, 2016. This reference period is aligned to the Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS) unemployment job search time period to provide a more accurate comparison of labor supply and labor demand.

The total number of Available Job Postings was roughly 33,400 in September 2012 and have risen to 40,000 in September 2016. Of the 40,000 job postings, about half are New Job Postings. About 80% are for full-time positions, 13% are part-time positions, 6.5% are contract positions and less than 1% are for internships.

Combined Food Preparation and Serving Workers, Including Fast Food, Maids and Housekeeping Cleaners, Maintenance and Repair Workers, General and Medical and Health Services Managers were the few occupations that saw a very slight year-to-year increase in postings.

Less than 1% of the available job postings, not reflected in this report, are for internship positions. The top internship postings are for Registered Nurses, Electricians, Public Relations Specialists and Market Research Analysts and Marketing Specialists.

The Hiring Scale views the difficulty-to-fill indicator on market conditions. It calculates a score for each position that the employer is trying to fill. Several factors affect the hiring scale score that include the supply of available candidates, the overall demand for employers for people in the position, the location, the occupation, local unemployment rates, job site source, offered pay level, etc. The Hiring Scale scores jobs from 1 to 99 based on their projected difficulty-to-recruit.

About half of the occupations on the Top 20 Job Postings list require less than an associate's degree. The hiring difficulty for most these occupations are also lower. The hiring difficulty for jobs that require a bachelor's degree, with or without related work experience, is higher in the region.

The Computer and Mathematical Occupations saw almost 1,360 less Available Job Postings compared to the same period last year. Computer Systems Analysts, Network and Computer Systems Administrators, Web Developers and Software Developers, Applications saw most of the decreases in postings. Approximately 90 percent of the postings under this category are for full-time positions and 40 are internship postings.
