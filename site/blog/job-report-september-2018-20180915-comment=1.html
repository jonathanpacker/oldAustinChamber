AUSTIN JOB POSTINGS REPORT SEPTEMBER 2018

TO SUBSCRIBE TO THIS MONTHLY REPORT, CLICK HERE.

AUSTIN - ROUND ROCK - SAN MARCOS MSA
(AUGUST 14, 2018 to SEPTEMBER 13, 2018)
austinchamber.com/jobpostings

(NOT FOR CITATION OR CIRCULATION WITHOUT PERMISSION)

INSIGHTS

  • There were approximately 41,600 Available Job Postings during the September 2018 monthly reporting period, 180 less postings when compared to September 2017.  8,800 or 21 percent of the Available Job Postings during the September reporting period fell under Computer and Mathematical Occupations, which also had the largest year-to-year increases in postings. 

  • Approximately 44 percent or about 18,200 of Available Job Postings are New Job Postings with Computer and Mathematical Occupations, Management Occupations, Healthcare Practitioners and Technical Occupations and Business and Financial Operations Occupations leading in this category.

  • 15,100 or 36% of all Available Job Postings pay an annual median wage of between $30,000 to $65,000 in the Austin MSA for September 2018. Of these 15,100 posted jobs in this category, 3,800 or 25 percent would require more than a high school diploma but less than a bachelor's degree while 9,800 or 58 percent would require a high school diploma or less. The top 10 posted occupations or approximately 5,700 postings, in this annual median wage range of $30,000 to $65,000 category, all require an entry level education of less than an associate's degree.

  • The region's Master Community Workforce Plan's top-line target is to ensure that in the five years between 2017-2021, an additional 1,800-plus Austin residents currently in poverty receive training, credentialing, and are placed in jobs paying at least $40,000 in technology, healthcare and skilled trades. Austin added 33,600 net new jobs in the 12 months ending in August, making Austin the 8th fastest growing major metro.

  • 60 percent of the approximate 17,200 unemployment insurance (UI) claimants in FY 2018 that are actively seeking a job in Travis County have less than an associate's degree, according to Workforce Solutions Capital Area.

  • 8,800 or 21 percent of the Available Job Postings during the September reporting period fell under Computer and Mathematical Occupations.

  • According to the Travis County Poverty Brief, the American Community Survey 2012-2016 data shows that 32 percent or 355,874 of Travis County residents have an income under 200% of the poverty threshold. 

  • More jobs ahead. Companies have recently announced new location or expansion plans in the Austin MSA, representing almost 660 jobs. The expansion list includes Walmart, Summit Stoneworks, Skills Fund, H-E-B, Four Hands, FirstClose, Facebook, Bungalo and Aeglea BioTherapeutics . Employers such as Jasper PIM (Toronto, Canada), Essentium (College Station, TX) and Clear (New York NY) are the companies that have announced new location plans in Austin.

The following job market report is designed to provide insight into current employer demands, skills and occupations in the Austin MSA.

There were approximately 41,600 Available Job Postings during the September 2018 monthly reporting period, 180 less postings when compared to September 2017.  8,800 or 21 percent of the Available Job Postings during the September reporting period fell under Computer and Mathematical Occupations which also had the largest year-to-year increases in postings. 

The Business and Financial Operations Occupations and Management Occupations also had significant increases in year-to-year postings.

The Major Occupational Groups that saw more than 400 year-to-year increases in Available Job Postings, when compared to September 2017 are Computer and Mathematical Occupations and Management Occupations. 

Approximately 44 percent or about 18,200 of job postings are New Job Postings with Computer and Mathematical Occupations, Management Occupations, Healthcare Practitioners and Technical Occupations and Business and Financial Operations Occupations leading in this category.

According to the Texas Workforce Commission, the Austin MSA reported an unemployment rate of 3.0% or 35,800 unemployed in August 2018, with 800 less people unemployed when compared to the previous month. The unemployment rate was 3.3% or 37,900 unemployed in August 2017. The current labor force for the Austin MSA is 1,179,200, an increase of 27,800 into our regions' labor force, when compared to the same time last year.

TOP 20 JOB POSTINGS AND HIRING DIFFICULTY

The chart below lists the top 20 unique available job postings and its hiring difficulty in the Austin – Round Rock – San Marcos MSA from August 14, 2018, to September 13, 2018.Source: Wanted Technologies and the Bureau of Labor Statistics

The Hiring Scale views the difficulty-to-fill indicator on market conditions. It calculates a score for each position that the employer is trying to fill. Several factors affect the hiring scale score that includes the supply of available candidates, the overall demand for employers for people in the position, the location, the occupation, local unemployment rates, job site source, offered pay level, etc. The Hiring Score scores jobs from 1 to 99 based on their projected difficulty-to-recruit.

Occupations that post a Hiring Difficulty of over 80 are technology-related jobs and/or require management experience such as Information Technology Project Managers, Computer Systems Engineers/Architects, Web Developers, Marketing Managers and Software Quality Assurance and Engineers.
