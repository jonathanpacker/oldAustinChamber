14 Additional Nonstop Flights Since January 1

14 Additional Nonstop Flights Since January 1

Posted By Greater Austin Chamber | Mar 31, 2017
Transportation Print Article Share

Executives tell us that they need more direct flights to key business destinations to grow their companies. Since the beginning of the year, the Austin-Bergstrom International Airport (ABIA) has added 14 nonstop flights to now offer 60 nonstop trips overall.

The Austin Chamber of Commerce created the Air Service Task Force in 2008 to partner with the ABIA and increase the number of direct US and international flights to Austin. The task force, chaired by Dell executive Doug Driskill, is comprised of industry, education, and aviation partners that support improved infrastructure to the airport, nonstop flights, and numerous carriers to advance the Austin-Bergstrom International Airport (ABIA).

For more information on how you can help with Austin’s growing air service demands, contact Drew Scheberle, Senior Vice President of advocacy, education, and talent development.

The additional nonstop flights include:

Allegiant Airlines
Allegiant Airlines launched new nonstop service between the ABIA and Tampa/St. Petersburg, Cleveland, Indianapolis, Destin and Ft. Walton Beach. The airline will begin flights to Destin/Ft. Walton Beach on May 4 and flights to Indianapolis on May 19. Flights to Tampa/St. Petersburg and Cleveland began February 17. The four additional new flights will bring Allegiant’s list of nonstop flights to 10.

Alaska Airlines
Alaska Airlines will start nonstop service flights to San Jose on August 27 and San Diego on August 28. The two additional flights bring Alaska’s list of nonstop flights to four.

Delta Airlines
Delta Airlines began nonstop flights to Raleigh-Durham on March 9. The airline will provide nonstop flights to Seattle on June 12. The additional flights bring Delta’s list of nonstop flights to eight.

Frontier Airlines
Frontier Airlines will begin nonstop flights to Washington DC on April 21 and San Diego on April 23. The additional flights bring Frontier’s nonstop flights to five.

Southwest Airlines
Southwest Airlines launched a nonstop trip to Kansas City on March 13. The airline will begin nonstop flights to Pensacola and Panama City on June 4. The additional three flights bring Southwest’s nonstop flights list to 34.

Sun Country Airlines
Sun Country Airlines will begin nonstop flights to Minneapolis-St Paul on August 31. The new addition brings Sun Country’s nonstop flight list to one.

In addition, Aeromexico began daily nonstop service between the ABIA and Mexico City. The route began as a four-day service in November 2016 and has grown to become a daily nonstop route.
