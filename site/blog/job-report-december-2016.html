AUSTIN JOB POSTINGS REPORT DECEMBER 2016

AUSTIN - ROUND ROCK - SAN MARCOS MSA
(NOVEMBER 14, 2016 to DECEMBER 13, 2016)
austinchamber.com/jobpostings

INSIGHTS

  • Approximately 35,700 Available Job Postings during the monthly reporting period, a decrease of 2,500 postings compared to the same period last year. 
  • Largest year-to-year decreases in the Sales and Related Occupations, Computer and Mathematical Occupations followed by Business and Financial Operations Occupations.
  • Several companies recently announced new location or expansion plans in Austin, involving more than 1,930 jobs. The list includes Sync1 Systems, Creative Alignments, Facebook, Intersys Consulting, Ledcor Technical Services, Manna Freight Systems, Onit, Performance Services, Samsung Austin Semiconductor, Snap Kitchen and Superior HealthPlan (Centene Corp.).

The following job market report is designed to provide insight into current employer demands, skills and occupations in the Austin MSA.

There were approximately 35,700 Available Job Postings during the December 2016 monthly reporting period, a decrease of about 2,500 Available Job Postings compared to December 2015. About 6,500 or 18 percent of the Available Job Postings during the November reporting period fell under Computer and Mathematical Occupations.

Other occupational groups not reflected in this report that account for another 3,500 Available Job Openings are Architecture and Engineering Occupations (3%), Construction and Extraction Occupations (3%), Healthcare Support Occupations (2%) and Education, Training, and Library Occupations (2%), representing approximately 10% of Available Job Postings.

Building and Grounds Cleaning and Maintenance Occupations and Healthcare Practioners and Technical Occupations are the two occupational groups that saw an increase of 150 or more postings compared to the same time last year.

According to the Texas Workforce Commission, the Austin MSA reported an unemployment rate of 3.0% or 33,700 unemployed in November 2016. The Austin MSA reported an annual job growth rate of 2.0% or 19,500 net new jobs in November 2016, down from 2.20% or 21,200 net new jobs in October 2016. Austin dropped from being the 20th fastest growing major metro to 25th place in November 2016. Education and Health Services added the most jobs (4.3% or 8,800) in the past year. The fastest growing industry was Construction and Natural Resources (6.6% or 3,800). Manufacturing jobs declined by 2.7% or 23,400, over the last 12 months.

Austin's total personal income grew by 7.2% in 2015 and has the 4th fastest growth rate among major metros, after San Jose, Denver and San Francisco. Austin has seen strong gains in average earnings per job at $60,805 in 2015, which is up 4.4% over 2014. 

Approximately 65% of unemployment insurance (UI) claimants that are actively seeking a job in Travis County have less than an associate's degree, according to Workforce Solutions Capital Area.

Source: The Conference Board Help Wanted OnLine ® (HWOL)

Note: The data for December 2016 would be the sum of all posted ads from November 14, 2016 to December 13, 2016. This reference period is aligned to the Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS) unemployment job search time period to provide a more accurate comparison of labor supply and labor demand.

The total number of Available Job Postings was 30,360 in December 2012 and have risen to 35,700 in December 2016. Of the 35,700 job postings, about half are New Job Postings. Of the Available Job Postings, about 79% are for full-time positions, 12% are part-time positions, 8% are contract positions and less than 1% are for internships.

Less than 1% or 400 of the Available Job Postings, not reflected in this report, are for internship positions. This is an approximate 30 percent year-to-year decrease. 

TOP 20 JOB POSTINGS AND HIRING DIFFICULTY

The chart below lists the top 20 unique available job postings and its hiring difficulty in the Austin – Round Rock – San Marcos MSA from November 14, 2016 to December 13, 2016.

Source: Wanted Technologies and the Bureau of Labor Statistics

The Hiring Scale views the difficulty-to-fill indicator on market conditions. It calculates a score for each position that the employer is trying to fill. Several factors affect the hiring scale score that include the supply of available candidates, the overall demand for employers for people in the position, the location, the occupation, local unemployment rates, job site source, offered pay level, etc. The Hiring Scale scores jobs from 1 to 99 based on their projected difficulty-to-recruit.

Almost all the occupations, except Heavy and Tractor-Trailer Truck Drivers and Web Developers, that indicate a difficulty-to-fill level of 80 or more would require a Bachelor's degree as the typical education needed for entry. It is easier to hire Computer Systems Analysts in our region, when compared nationally.
