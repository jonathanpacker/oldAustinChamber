AUSTIN JOB POSTINGS REPORT APRIL 2017

AUSTIN - ROUND ROCK - SAN MARCOS MSA
(MARCH 14, 2017 to APRIL 13, 2017)
austinchamber.com/jobpostings

INSIGHTS

  • Lots of hiring. 9,200 more people employed between February 2017 and March 2017.

  • Many job openings, but fewer than a year ago. 39,600 Available Job Postings during March 2017, or 4,900 less compared to April 2016. 

  • Job postings in April 2017 is lower than April of past three years. The Austin MSA saw more than 40,000 Available Job Postings in 2014, 2015 and 2016 but is below 40,000 in April 2017.

  • Demand for Tech talent remains high. Software Developers-Applications replaced Registered Nurses as the top posted occupation in March 2017 and again in April 2017. The annual median wage for Software Developers-Applications in the Austin MSA increased from $99,420 to $105,150, according to the recently released data by the BLS.

  • Plenty of middle wage jobs available. Ten of the Top 20 occupations posted require less than an associate's degree as the typical education needed for entry and all but two of the top occupations posted pay an annual median wage of $30,000 or more in the Austin MSA.

  • Unemployment tops 41,300. According to the Texas Workforce Commission, the Austin MSA reported an unemployment rate of 3.6% or 41,300 unemployed in March 2017. This represents an decrease of 700 unemployed compared to February 2017 which had a 3.7% unemployment rate or 42,000 unemployed. In February and March of 2017, the number of unemployed was greater than the number of Available Job Postings, which has not been the case since March 2015.

  • More jobs ahead. Several companies recently announced new location or expansion plans in the Austin MSA including about 725 jobs. The list includes Accent Food Services, BigCommerce, ModSquad, MW Builders, Mythic, Peregine Semiconductor, TG, TurnKey Vacation Rentals and Babson Diagnostics.

  • Austin net job growth ranks 10th. The Austin MSA reported an annual job growth rate of 3.3% or 32,700 net new jobs in the 12 months ending in March making Austin the 10th fastest growing major metro. 

The following job market report is designed to provide insight into current employer demands, skills and occupations in the Austin MSA.

There were approximately 39,600 Available Job Postings during the April 2017 monthly reporting period, a decrease of about 4,900 Available Job Postings compared to April 2016. About 7,400 or 19 percent of the Available Job Postings during the April reporting period fell under Computer and Mathematical Occupations. Slightly more than half of the Available Job Postings are New Job Postings for April 2017.

The Major Occupational Groups that saw the largest decline in the volume of Available Job Postings compared to April 2016 are Office and Administrative Support Occupations, Sales and Related Occupations and Food Preparation and Serving Related Occupations. Computer and Mathematical Occupations had the largest volume of New Job Postings for April 2017.

According to the Texas Workforce Commission, the Austin MSA reported an unemployment rate of 3.6% or 41,300 unemployed in March 2017. This represents a slight decrease of 700 unemployed compared to February 2017 which had a 3.7% unemployment rate or 42,000 unemployed. In March 2016, the unemployment rate was 3.1% or 34,400 unemployed. Approximately 65% of unemployment insurance (UI) claimants that are actively seeking a job in Travis County have less than an associate's degree, according to Workforce Solutions Capital Area.

The Austin MSA reported an annual job growth rate of 3.3% or 32,700 net new jobs in the 12 months ending in March, making Austin the 10th fastest growing major metro. Education and Healthcare added the most jobs (5.5% or 6,300) in the last 12 months - while the fastest growing industry was Transportation, Warehousing and Utilities (6.4% or 1,100 jobs).

The U.S. News & World Report named Austin as the "Best Place to Live in the U.S." in 2017. Savills calls Austin "one of the 22 Cities at the Forefront of Global Tech" while SmartAsset named Austin as one of the "10 Best Cities to Raise a Family". Austin also comes in 3rd on Gallups' "Good Jobs Rate" ranking based on the percentage of adults who work for an employer full-time. Indeed ranked Austin No. 4 on its "25 Best Cities for Job Seekers" list.

TOP 20 JOB POSTINGS

The chart below lists the top 20 unique available job postings in the Austin – Round Rock – San Marcos MSA from March 14, 2017 to April 13, 2017.
Source: The Conference Board Help Wanted OnLine ® (HWOL) and Bureau of Labor Statistics, May 2016

Software Developers-Applications is again, the top occupation posted in the Austin MSA and its annual median wage increased from $99,420 to $105,150 according to the latest data released by the BLS.

Management Analysts, Computer Systems Engineers/Architects, Software Quality Assurance Engineers and Testers, Marketing Managers, Information Technology Project Managers, Software Developers-Applications and Pharmacy Technicians all saw a 70 or more year-to-year increase in postings. First-Line Supervisors of Food Preparation and Serving Workers, Registered Nurses, Customer Service Representatives, Retail Salespersons, Tellers and Web Developers saw a 150 or more year-to-year decrease in postings.

TOP 20 JOB POSTINGS AND HIRING DIFFICULTY

The chart below lists the top 20 unique available job postings and its hiring difficulty in the Austin – Round Rock – San Marcos MSA from March 14, 2017 to April 13, 2017.

Source: Wanted Technologies and the Bureau of Labor Statistics

The Hiring Scale views the difficulty-to-fill indicator on market conditions. It calculates a score for each position that the employer is trying to fill. Several factors affect the hiring scale score that include the supply of available candidates, the overall demand for employers for people in the position, the location, the occupation, local unemployment rates, job site source, offered pay level, etc. The Hiring Scale scores jobs from 1 to 99 based on their projected difficulty-to-recruit.

Ten of the Top 20 occupations posted require less than an associate's degree as the typical education needed for entry and all but two of the top occupations posted pay an annual median wage of $30,000 or more in the Austin MSA.

The Austin MSA has a higher hiring difficulty for Maids and Housekeeping Cleaners, Management Analysts, Maintenance and Repair Workers-General and Registered Nurses when compared nationally. Executive Secretaries and Executive Administrative Assistants, Computer Systems Analysts, Computer User Support Specialists and Heavy and Tractor-Trailer Truck Drivers are easier to recruit here when compared nationwide. Customer Service Representatives, Retail Salespersons, Executive Secretaries and Executive Administrative Assistants and Computer Systems Analysts are the easier-to-hire occupations in the Austin MSA.

Computer Systems Engineers/Architects saw the largest year-to-year increase in Available Jobs Postings, followed by Software Quality Assurance Engineers and Testers, Information Technology Project Managers and Software Developers-Applications.

The annual median wages in this occupational category saw an increase of $3,490 from $79,980 in 2015 to $83,470 in 2016.

Computer and Mathematical Occupations saw approximately 110 less Available Job Postings compared to the same period last year.

Several factors affect the hiring scale score that include the supply of available candidates, the overall demand for employers for people in the position, the location, the occupation, local unemployment rates, job site source, offered pay level, etc. The Hiring Scale scores jobs from 1 to 99 based on their projected difficulty-to-recruit.

Companies such as HCA-The Healthcare Company, Austin Medical Center and St. David's Medical Center have seen a 100 or more year-to-year increase in postings under this category.

Most of the year-to-year increases in this occupational category are for Pharmacy Technicians, though the demand is relatively easy to fill in our region and typically requires a high school diploma plus moderate-on-the-job training. Compared nationally, the Austin MSA has a higher hiring difficulty for Dental Hygienists, Surgical Technologists and Cardiovascular Technologists and Technicians.
