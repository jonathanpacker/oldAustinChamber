AUSTIN JOB POSTINGS REPORT AUGUST 2016

AUSTIN - ROUND ROCK - SAN MARCOS MSA
(JULY 14, 2016 to AUGUST 13, 2016)
austinchamber.com/jobpostings
The following job market report is designed to provide insight into current employer demands, skills and occupations in the Austin MSA.

INSIGHTS

  • Approximately 41,500 Available Job Postings during the monthly reporting period, a decrease of 5,300 postings compared to the same period last year. 
  • Largest year-to-year decreases in the Computer and Mathematical Occupations, followed by Healthcare Practitioners and Technical Occupations, Office and Administrative Support Occupations and Sales and Related Occupations.
  • Several companies recently announced new location or expansion plans in Austin, involving more than 1,700 jobs. The list includes Apple, Abel Design Group, Aliya Financial, Best Buy, Data Foundry, FloSports, Magic Leap, Pedernales Electric Cooperative, Seed Sumo, TenantCloud, ThousandEyes, Twitch, uShip, Alpine Consulting, C3 Presents, Convey,Facebook, Indeed, Ketra,Kuka Robotics, National Domestic Violence Hotline, Outdoor Voices, ProCore Technologies, Q2 Holdings, Samsung Austin Semiconductor, ShipStation, TriNet, World First, WP Engine.

There were approximately 41,500 Available Job Postings during the August 2016 monthly reporting period, a decrease of about 5,300 Available Job Postings compared to August of 2015. About 7,000 or 17 percent of the available postings during the August reporting period fell under Computer and Mathematical Occupations.

USA Today ranked Austin 9th on the "American Cities Adding The Most Jobs". The Kauffman Foundation named Austin 1st in the "Top 25 Cities For Startup Activity, 2016". According to CBRE, Austin is ranked 5th in the "Scoring Tech Talent" list for 2016.

According to the Texas Workforce Commission, the Austin MSA reported an unemployment rate of 3.6% or 39,800 unemployed in July 2016. The Austin MSA reported an annual job growth rate of 3.9% or 37,800 net new jobs in July 2016, making Austin the 3rd fastest growing major metro in the nation. Professional and Business Services added the most jobs (7,900 or 4.9%) in the past year. The fastest growing industries are Construction and Natural Resources (12.1%) and Wholesale Trade (8.9%).

Approximately 65% of unemployment insurance (UI) claimants that are actively seeking a job in Travis County have less than an associate's degree, according to Workforce Solutions Capital Area.

TOP 20 JOB POSTINGS

The chart below lists the top 20 unique available job postings in the Austin – Round Rock – San Marcos MSA from July 14, 2016 to August 13, 2016.

Source: The Conference Board Help Wanted OnLine ® (HWOL) and Bureau of Labor Statistics, May 2015

First-Line Supervisors of Retail Sales Workers, First-Line Supervisors of Food Preparation and Serving Workers, Management Analysts and Maids and Housekeeping Cleaners were the few occupations that saw a slight year-to-year increase in postings.

About 340 or less than 1% of the available job postings, not reflected in this report, are for internship positions. The top internship postings are for Electricians, Registered Nurses, Public Relations Specialists, Market Research Analysts and Marketing Specialists and Pharmacists. Companies like Walmart have posted internships for Pharmacists while several non-profit organizations have posted for Public Relations Specialists. The occupational group, Healthcare Practitioners and Technical Occupations, posted the most internship positions.

The Hiring Scale views the difficulty-to-fill indicator on market conditions. It calculates a score for each position that the employer is trying to fill. Several factors affect the hiring scale score that include the supply of available candidates, the overall demand for employers for people in the position, the location, the occupation, local unemployment rates, job site source, offered pay level, etc. The Hiring Scale scores jobs from 1 to 99 based on their projected difficulty-to-recruit.

Half of the occupations on the Top 20 Job Postings list require less than an associate's degree. The hiring difficulty for most these occupations are also lower. The hiring difficulty for jobs that require a bachelor's degree, with or without related work experience, is higher in the region.

The Computer and Mathematical Occupations saw almost 1,500 less Available Job Postings compared to the same period last year. Approximately 90 percent of the postings under this category are for full-time positions.
