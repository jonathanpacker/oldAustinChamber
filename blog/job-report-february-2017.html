AUSTIN JOB POSTINGS REPORT FEBRUARY 2017

AUSTIN - ROUND ROCK - SAN MARCOS MSA
(JANUARY 14, 2017 to FEBRUARY 13, 2017)
austinchamber.com/jobpostings

INSIGHTS

  • Approximately 38,100 Available Job Postings during the monthly reporting period, a decrease of 5,700 postings compared to the same period last year. 

  • Largest year-to-year decreases in the Computer and Mathematical Occupations, Sales and Related Occupations, followed by Food Preparation and Serving Related Occupations.

  • Several companies recently announced new location or expansion plans in the Austin MSA, involving about 180 jobs. The list includes Coast Flight Training, Heartland Acoustics & Interiors, Purple, Oowisio, World First and YouEarnedIt.

  • The Austin MSA reported an annual job growth rate of 1.9% or 18,800 net new jobs in December 2016, down from 2.0% or 19,500 net new jobs in November 2016, according to the Texas Workforce Commission (TWC) and the U.S... Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS). Job growth in the Austin area is slowing. Austin’s job growth has dropped from 3rd place in July 2016 to its current 25th place among the 50 largest U.S. metro areas.

  • Education and health services added the most jobs (3,100 or 2.7%) in the past year - while the fastest growing industry was financial activities (4.8% or 2,600 jobs).

The following job market report is designed to provide insight into current employer demands, skills and occupations in the Austin MSA.

There were approximately 38,100 Available Job Postings during the February 2017 monthly reporting period, a decrease of about 5,700 Available Job Postings compared to February 2016. About 7,000 or 18 percent of the Available Job Postings during the February reporting period fell under Computer and Mathematical Occupations.

Other occupational groups not reflected in this report that account for another 3,900 Available Job Openings are Architecture and Engineering Occupations (3%), Building and Grounds Cleaning and Maintenance Occupations (3%), Arts, Design, Entertainment, Sports and Media Occupations (2%) and Healthcare Support Occupations (2%), representing approximately 10% of Available Job Postings. About 51% of all posted jobs are New Job Postings. 

According to the Texas Workforce Commission, the Austin MSA reported an unemployment rate of 3.2% or 35,500 unemployed in December 2016, an increase of 2,800 unemployed compared to December 2015.

The Austin metropolitan area added 18,800 net new jobs, or 1.9%, in the 12 months ending in December, according to Friday's release of payroll jobs numbers by the Texas Workforce Commission (TWC) and the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS). December appears to continue (numbers are preliminary) a drop-off in Austin’s rate of job growth that began in August or, arguably, earlier. 

Approximately 65% of unemployment insurance (UI) claimants that are actively seeking a job in Travis County have less than an associate's degree, according to Workforce Solutions Capital Area.

The chart below lists the top 20 unique available job postings in the Austin – Round Rock – San Marcos MSA from January 14, 2017 to February 13, 2017.

Office Clerks-General, Management Analysts, Pharmacy Technicians and Radiologic Technologists all saw a 60 or more year-to-year increase in postings.

Computer Systems Analysts, First-Line Supervisors of Food Preparation and Serving Workers, Web Developers, Computer User Support Specialists, Software Developers-Applications, Registered Nurses and Heavy and Tractor-Trailer Truck Drivers saw a 150 or more year-to-year decrease in postings.

TOP 20 JOB POSTINGS AND HIRING DIFFICULTY

The chart below lists the top 20 unique available job postings and its hiring difficulty in the Austin – Round Rock – San Marcos MSA from January 14, 2017 to February 13, 2017.

Source: Wanted Technologies and the Bureau of Labor Statistics

The Hiring Scale views the difficulty-to-fill indicator on market conditions. It calculates a score for each position that the employer is trying to fill. Several factors affect the hiring scale score that include the supply of available candidates, the overall demand for employers for people in the position, the location, the occupation, local unemployment rates, job site source, offered pay level, etc. The Hiring Scale scores jobs from 1 to 99 based on their projected difficulty-to-recruit.

The Austin MSA has a higher hiring difficulty for Maids and Housekeeping Cleaners, Management Analysts, Maintenance and Repair Workers-General, Sales Managers and Registered Nurses when compared nationally. Computer Systems Analysts, Computer User Support Specialists and Heavy and Tractor-Trailer Truck Drivers are easier to recruit here when compared nationwide.
